# POC Retrieval Round 5 — LLM-judge A/B (binary 0/1 vs graded 0-3)

**Run date:** 2026-04-27
**Status:** **Spike inconclusive on Cohen κ alone (3-run Δ = +0.069 ± 0.120 → not statistically distinguishable). HOWEVER, graded arm B provides Spearman rank-correlation signal (ρ = 0.38 ± 0.07) and fuzzy-negative discrimination that binary arm A cannot. Recommendation: adopt graded for the additional information, not for marginal κ improvement.**

## Motivation

R4 stalled because hand-annotated `expected_paths` reflects the annotator's bias more than retrieval quality (EVAL_DESIGN_NOTES Rule 3 + Limitations). ARCHITECTURE.md §9 Phase 3 Gate writes: "do not introduce cross-encoder reranking until graded-relevance LLM-judge eval is deployed."

R5 spikes the gate by running an LLM-judge over R4's existing 30 query × top-5 hits, comparing two arms:
- **arm A** — binary 0/1 verdict (matches ragas `LLMContextPrecisionWithoutReference` shape)
- **arm B** — graded 0-3 NIST TREC scale (0=non-rel, 1=tangential, 2=relevant, 3=canonical)

Both arms judge the SAME (query, hit-snippet) pairs from R4. Hand-score baseline = R4's `precision_at_5` thresholded at 0.5 binary.

## Implementation

`experiments/poc-retrieval/eval/`:
- `ragas_spike.py` — async runner, `asyncio.Semaphore(24)` + tenacity backoff
- `ragas_prompts.py` — code-retrieval-adapted prompts (ragas default assumes generated `answer`, CodeNexus is retrieval-only)
- Provider: **okaoi** Anthropic-Messages pool (3 keys × 30 parallel = 90 ceiling, MiniMax-M2.7)
- 30 queries × 5 top hits × 2 arms = 300 LLM calls/round
- Snippets resolved from `poc.db.symbols` table by `(path, name)` key (100% lookup hit)

### Why not vanilla ragas runtime

ragas 0.2 `LLMContextPrecisionWithoutReference` requires a `answer` field (generated by the RAG pipeline). CodeNexus is **retrieval-only** — there is no generated answer. We ported the LLM-judge methodology (binary verdict per ragas + 0-3 graded per NIST TREC) with prompts specific to (query, code-snippet) pairs, using the `anthropic` SDK directly. ragas dependency stays in `pyproject.toml` for posterity but its `evaluate()` is not invoked.

### Provider journey (recorded for future runs)

1. **codexcc.top minimax relay** (planned) — key 8 days old, 401 expired on first probe
2. **MiniMax 官方 anthropic** (`api.minimaxi.com/anthropic`, MiniMax-M2.5) — works but Token Plan rate-limits at concurrency 16 (40/300 = 13% 429 errors)

| **Δ(B@t2 − A) per-run** | — | — | — | — | [+0.208, 0.000, 0.000] |
| **Δ(B@t2 − A) mean ± std** | — | — | — | — | **+0.069 ± 0.120** |

**Honest reading**: Δ mean (+0.069) < std (0.120). **Arm B@t2 is not statistically distinguishable from arm A on Cohen κ.** The +0.21 in Run 1 is an outlier; Runs 2 & 3 are tied. With N=3 we cannot claim graded > binary on κ alone.

**Where arm B clearly wins**: Spearman ρ = 0.38 ± 0.07. Binary arm A has zero rank information by definition (κ ignores ordering). Graded gives a monotone signal that tracks hand precision — useful for reranker config tuning where rank position carries the result, not just hit/miss.

`@t2` = arm_B mean grade thresholded at ≥2 (relevant or canonical). This converts 0-3 graded to per-query precision_at_5 directly comparable to hand binary.

## What 0-3 graded buys vs binary

1. **Distinguishes "tangential" (1) from "non-relevant" (0)** — fuzzy-neg queries (A9/A10) score ~1-2 not 0-3, which thresholding ≥2 correctly excludes from "match" set, while still preserving Spearman signal for ranker tuning.
2. **Distinguishes "relevant" (2) from "canonical" (3)** — for reranker eval where finding the BEST match matters more than finding ANY match. Phase 3 reranker tuning needs this discrimination.
3. **Spearman ρ ~0.34 is informative even when κ is moderate** — graded scores correlate with hand precision monotonically; rank position carries information binary loses.

## What 0-3 graded does NOT solve

- **Axis-3 call-relation blind spot** (C7) — both arms miss because LLM judges score on snippet semantic similarity, not actual data-flow / call-graph relationships. Static analysis or program-slicing would help here, not better prompts.
- **NEG-encoding artifacts** (C9) — when hand-score uses non-standard encoding (e.g. precision_at_5=1.0 for correctly-rejected NEG queries), LLM-judge can't unambiguously interpret. Fix is on the eval-data side, not the judge side.
- **Reason for stochasticity** — same prompt, same model, temp=0.0, but κ varies ±0.07 across runs. MiniMax-M2.7 not fully deterministic at temp=0; require N≥3 seed averaging for confident verdict.

## Recommendation

**Adopt arm B (0-3 graded) as Phase 3 Gate primary metric — but for Spearman ρ, not κ.** Honest scoping:

1. **κ@t2 is at parity with binary arm A** (Δ = +0.069 ± 0.120 across N=3 runs). Do not claim graded "wins" on agreement-with-hand alone.
2. **Spearman ρ = 0.38 ± 0.07 is the load-bearing signal** — graded scores carry monotone information binary discards. This is what reranker tuning needs: which of two reranker configs produces results that LLM-judge ranks higher, not just whether they cross a binary threshold.
3. **Report (mean κ@t2, mean Spearman ρ, std) with N≥3 seeds always** — single-run κ noise is ±0.07, single-run Spearman noise is ±0.07. Phase 3 Gate decisions on reranker config require seed averaging, not point estimates.
4. **Axis-3 (call-relation) queries excluded from Phase 3 Gate headline metric** until static-analysis-augmented eval is added — both binary and graded LLM judges are blind here, forcing them into the metric punishes good retrieval.
5. **arm B catches EVAL Rule 2 fuzzy-negative bug consistently** (A9 across all 3 runs) — qualitative win independent of the κ tie.
6. **EVAL_DESIGN_NOTES.md Rule 6 candidate text** (for user review): "LLM-judge graded 0-3 with N≥3 seed averaging is primary metric; report (mean κ@t2, mean Spearman ρ, std). Hand-annotated `expected_paths` is cross-check only, kept for cold-start and reproducibility audit."
